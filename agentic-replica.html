Agentic Replica

Mapping the as-is workflow of human employees to create a factual baseline for AI agent development.

The Approach

Agentic Replica focuses on understanding the existing role without proposing changes—treating the job as a "black box" to catalog inputs, transformations, and outputs. The goal is to create a factual baseline for white-collar roles like analysts, managers, or coordinators, emphasizing aspects AI agents could observe or replicate.

This approach draws from systems thinking and traditional job analysis but prioritizes non-intrusive, data-driven methods to avoid bias from self-reporting. Rather than interviewing employees about what they think they do, we observe what actually happens.

The IPO Model

The analysis yields an Input-Process-Output (IPO) diagram, highlighting AI-replicable patterns:

Inputs

Data, requests, or resources entering the system

Transformations

Cognitive or procedural work, including tacit knowledge

Outputs

Deliverables, decisions, or communications produced

Core Process

A 4-step iterative cycle, starting with passive data collection and ending with validation. Use this for quick, objective insights, involving HR, IT, or the employee minimally.

1

Identify Inputs

Catalog what flows into the role: emails, meetings, data feeds (e.g., CRM updates), documents, or queries from colleagues. Review org charts to trace upstream sources and handoffs.

Example: A financial analyst's inputs might include market data APIs, stakeholder requests via Slack, and regulatory alerts.

2

Map Transformations

Break down how inputs are processed: decision rules, tools used (e.g., Excel for modeling), and tacit elements like judgment calls. Use black-box inference—analyze logs or assets without deep employee involvement.

Example: Examine spreadsheets to infer formulas or logic; monitor system usage (ethically) to see time on apps like email vs. analysis tools.

3

Document Outputs

List what emerges: reports, recommendations, approvals, or updated records. Trace downstream impacts. Quantify where possible (e.g., frequency of outputs via email archives).

Example: A marketer's campaign draft leads to team feedback loops, approval chains, and published content.

4

Validate and Refine

Cross-check with light interviews or shadow sessions to confirm the black-box model. Use LLMs to simulate based on captured data.

Example: "Based on these email samples, infer the transformation steps" — using AI to validate your model against real artifacts.

Optional / Enhancing Techniques

Secondary methods to deepen your research when the core process needs supplementation:

Digital Tracing

Ethically monitor computer activity (e.g., app logs, keystroke patterns) to quantify transformations without observation bias.

Artifact Audit

Dive into working assets (spreadsheets, docs) to externalize tacit knowledge, using cognitive mapping to verbalize implied rules.

JTBD Lite

Apply a simplified Jobs to Be Done to frame transformations as "hired jobs" (e.g., "transform raw data into actionable insights"), focusing on current behaviors only.

Enables

  • Automation opportunity identification
  • Baseline for reengineering efforts
  • Training data collection strategy
