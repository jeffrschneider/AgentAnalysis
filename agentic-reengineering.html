Agentic Reengineering

Envisioning how job areas can be optimized with AI agents—restructuring workflows for efficiency, scalability, and innovation.

The Approach

Agentic Reengineering shifts to speculative redesign, imagining how the job area could be optimized with AI agents—potentially restructuring workflows, roles, and handoffs for efficiency, scalability, or innovation.

Inspired by Business Process Reengineering (BPR), this approach assumes radical changes like automating handoffs or augmenting human creativity, while considering ethical factors such as job displacement. The approach is forward-looking, using simulations and benchmarks to prototype AI-driven alternatives.

This process is kept separate from the current state to avoid conflating "as-is" with "to-be"—ensuring clear thinking about what's possible versus what exists today.

Task Decomposition Model

From the black-box model, tasks are categorized into three types:

Automatable

Tasks agents can handle autonomously

  • • Data aggregation
  • • Report generation
  • • Email triage
  • • Status updates

Augmentable

Tasks enhanced by AI assistance

  • • AI-assisted decisions
  • • Draft generation
  • • Research synthesis
  • • Anomaly detection

Human-Only

Tasks requiring human judgment

  • • Ethical decisions
  • • Relationship building
  • • Creative direction
  • • Final accountability

Core Process

A 5-step creative cycle, building on current state data but emphasizing experimentation. Involve cross-functional teams (e.g., AI devs, domain experts) in workshops.

1

Envision Redesign Goals

Define objectives based on pain points from the current state (e.g., reduce handoff delays). Use BPR principles: question assumptions, integrate processes end-to-end, and prioritize customer/value outcomes.

Key questions: What takes too long? Where do errors occur? What would 10x improvement look like?

2

Decompose and Reassign Tasks

From the black-box model, break tasks into automatable, augmentable, and human-only categories. Imagine multi-agent systems for complex workflows.

Example: A "planner agent" coordinating with "executor agents" for a marketing campaign workflow.

3

Simulate AI-Enabled Workflows

Prototype with LLMs: prompt to reimagine roles. Model handoffs as agent-to-agent or agent-to-human. Use agent-building tools like AutoGen for complex prototypes.

Prompt example: "Redesign a marketing coordinator's workflow with AI agents handling email triage and content drafting."

4

Evaluate Feasibility and Impact

Assess using metrics like automation potential (e.g., via McKinsey frameworks) or ROI simulations. Test prototypes on sample data from current assets.

Metrics to consider: Time saved, error reduction, scalability factor, human satisfaction, cost delta.

5

Iterate and Document the To-Be Model

Refine based on simulations, creating blueprints for implementation (e.g., agent specs: tools, reasoning chains). Address risks like AI hallucinations through human oversight loops.

Output: Reengineered process map showing streamlined flows (e.g., AI automating 60% of transformations).

Optional / Enhancing Techniques

Secondary methods to enrich your future state vision:

Scenario Planning

Use foresight methods to explore multiple futures (e.g., full automation vs. hybrid), incorporating trends like advanced LLMs.

Multi-Agent Benchmarks

Draw from AI research (e.g., AgentBench) to test redesigned workflows in simulated environments before deployment.

Ethical Review

Integrate impact assessments to balance efficiency with job equity, ensuring reengineering enhances rather than eliminates roles.

Multi-Agent Architecture Patterns

When redesigning workflows, consider these common multi-agent patterns:

Planner-Executor

One agent plans and delegates, others execute specific tasks. Good for complex workflows with clear decomposition.

Pipeline

Sequential handoffs where each agent's output feeds the next. Ideal for linear processes with clear stages.

Peer Collaboration

Agents work as equals, sharing information and coordinating dynamically. Suited for creative or research tasks.

Human-in-the-Loop

Human oversees and intervenes at key decision points. Essential for high-stakes or novel situations.
